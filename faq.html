FAQs

Project Amanat is a patient monitoring and ventilation system built with Arduino and Blynk IoT. It tracks vital signs and environmental conditions to promote patient well-being and comfort.

Our product has 2 different functions. Patient Monitoring is the first. It monitors heart rate and body temperature, sending the data wirelessly to a smartphone or tablet app via Blynk. Environmental Control is the second. It uses a DHT sensor to record temperature and humidity. Based on programmed thresholds, the system can turn on/off a fan to maintain a comfortable environment.

Currently, the system focuses on heart rate and body temperature.

Yes, with additional sensors, it can be expanded to monitor blood oxygen, respiration rate, or other relevant data points.

The Blynk app provides a user-friendly interface to view real-time data in a clear and organised way.

No, it's for monitoring purposes only and should not replace professional medical care.

Blynk offers data encryption options, but it's crucial to research and implement appropriate security measures for your specific use case.

The system can control a small fan with an appropriate power supply through a relay module.

The system compares temperature and humidity readings to pre-set thresholds. If these thresholds are exceeded, the fan will turn on to regulate the environment.

The current version controls the fan on/off state. With additional components, fan speed control might be possible.

Yes, the system can be customized by adding sensors, adjusting control parameters, and incorporating features like alarms for abnormal readings.
